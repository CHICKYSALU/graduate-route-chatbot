FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Everything students usually ask before booking. If your question isn't here, use the chat in the corner or send it through the contact form.

Do I need to book an Initial Consultation first?

If you want support with a full application from scratch, yes. For complete application support, end-to-end scholarship support, undergraduate applications, postgraduate applications, or anything that requires building a full strategy with you, the Initial Consultation is the first step. For focused services like essay ideation, essay reviews, academic CV reviews, mock interviews, or interview preparation, you can book directly.

Is the Initial Consultation paid?

Yes. The Initial Consultation is a paid one-on-one strategy session designed to give you clarity before you commit to a larger application route. All services are paid.

What happens after I book an Initial Consultation?

We use the session to understand your profile, including academic background, goals, target programmes, scholarship plans, funding needs, timeline, and any concerns about the process. After that, we recommend the support route that fits you best, whether a complete end-to-end package, support for one scholarship, undergraduate or university application support, or a more focused service.

Can I book only one service instead of a full package?

Yes. If you only need help with one part of the process, you can book focused services such as essay ideation, essay review, academic CV review, interview preparation, or a mock interview. If your application needs broader planning, for example shortlisting universities or building a full timeline, we may recommend starting with an Initial Consultation first.

Do you guarantee admissions or scholarships?

No. Admissions and scholarships are competitive, and final decisions are always made by universities, scholarship bodies, and selection committees. What we can promise is honest, thoughtful, experienced support that helps you build a stronger application strategy and present your profile as clearly as possible.

Do you write essays for students?

No. Your essays need to come from you, your experiences, ideas, goals, and voice. We do not invent stories or write application material on your behalf. We guide you: helping you understand the prompt, shape your structure, and strengthen your narrative.

What is the difference between an Essay Ideation Session and an Essay Review?

An Essay Ideation Session is for when you do not know what to write yet, when you're unsure how to approach the prompt or what examples to use. An Essay Review is for when you already have a complete draft. If your essay is still unfinished or only at the idea stage, we recommend starting with ideation first.

Do you review incomplete essays?

No. Essay reviews are only for complete drafts. If yours is still incomplete or scattered, an Essay Ideation Session is the better place to start so we can help you build the right direction before reviewing the writing.

What is included in Complete End-to-End Application Support?

The full Graduate Route experience across the entire application journey, from finalising programmes to preparing materials and interview readiness. Depending on your route, this can include programme shortlisting, scholarship planning, essay support, SOP support, CV review, recommendation guidance, application form review, interview preparation, and final checks before submission.

What is the difference between Complete End-to-End Support and Single Scholarship End-to-End Support?

Complete End-to-End Support is for students applying across a wider cycle, which may include multiple universities, scholarships, or countries. Single Scholarship End-to-End Support is focused on one specific scholarship or funding opportunity, with full support for that one application. If you're not sure which fits, the Initial Consultation helps guide you.

Do you support both undergraduate and postgraduate applications?

Yes. For undergraduate applicants, we help with country guidance, university shortlisting, personal statements, supplemental essays, activities sections, and application planning. For postgraduate applicants, we help with university applications, scholarships, SOPs, CVs, recommendation strategy, and interview preparation. Every route is shaped around the student's level, goals, and target country.

Which countries do you support?

We support applications globally, depending on the student's goals and the requirements of the application. We've worked with students applying to the UK, Europe, the US, Australia, the Middle East, and other international destinations.

How early should I start?

As early as possible. Strong applications need time, to shortlist properly, prepare strong documents, request recommendations, and write better essays without last-minute panic. We also stop taking on clients too close to major deadlines, since meaningful support takes time. If you're unsure whether you have enough time, ask sooner rather than later.

Do you help with recommendation letters?

Yes, with recommendation letter strategy, choosing the right recommenders, preparing useful information for them, and drafting professional emails when needed. We do not write the letters themselves, the final letter must come from the person recommending you.

Do you submit applications for students?

No. We help you prepare, review, and strengthen your materials, but the final application must remain yours. You're responsible for checking all information and submitting the final application yourself.

Do you help with application forms?

Yes, where relevant. For certain services and packages, we can review application form responses to make sure they're clear, consistent, and aligned with the rest of your application. You remain responsible for entering accurate information and submitting the final form.

Is The Graduate Route an editing service or a mentorship service?

It depends on what you book. For focused services, we may work on one specific part of your application. For larger packages, the process is much more like mentorship, helping you think through your story, strategy, and overall application direction. Across all services, our role is to guide, structure, review, and refine, while keeping your voice and goals at the centre.

I am not sure which service I need. What should I do?

Start with the Initial Consultation. If you're unsure whether you need full application support, one scholarship route, undergraduate guidance, essay support, CV review, or interview preparation, the consultation will help you figure that out.

What is The Graduate Route's approach to application support?

We believe strong applications should be honest, personal, and genuinely reflective of the student. We do not write essays for you, invent experiences, exaggerate achievements, or submit applications on your behalf. Instead, we help you understand your own story more clearly and present it with more strategy, structure, and confidence.
